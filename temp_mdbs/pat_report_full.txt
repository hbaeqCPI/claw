PCT Deadline Changes
In this update, we are adjusting PCT Deadlines for better clarity and flexibility.
Notably, National Phase filing deadlines have now been added to generate on the designated PCT applications, in addition to the international WO applications. The actions on the WO international applications are also being renamed.
The 30-month filing deadlines "Chapter I Due (Extended)" and "Chapter II Due (Nat. Filing)" have been consolidated into "Nat. Filing Due - 30 Months," in keeping with the existing "Nat. Filing Due - 31 Months."
Going forward, the action "Chapter II/Exam Fee Due" will be "Demand Due (Chapter II)."
The 20-month action "Chapter I Due (Nat. Filing)" has been removed from the international WO record, as this earlier requirement can now be handled directly on Luxembourg and Tanzania if desired.

Manual Updates

Records in the Action Type table under the Auxiliary or Maintenance Menu in your system are not automatically modified. They will need to be adjusted by a person responsible for data entry. Go to the Auxiliary or Maintenance Menu in your system and make the changes to the Action Types below if you do not already have them.

Remarks: Where a European patent claims priority to a previous application, a copy of any search results for the priority application must be submitted with the European Patent Office upon filing or entry to the European regional phase. Country: Albania (AL)

ORD - Utility Patent Filing

Law Highlights Conventions: Paris Convention, PCT, WIPO, WTO TRIPS, Patent Law Treaty, European Patent Convention
Filing Requirements: 1. Request for the grant of a patent with applicant and inventor details 2. Specification with description and claims 3. Formal drawings 4. Power of Attorney may be requested by the Office. (Kluwer IP Law; retrieved 22 Sep 2021) (Katzarov Manual of Industrial Property; retrieved 22 Sep 2021) Documents should be in Albanian or have an Albanian translation submitted within 2 months of the filing date. (Kluwer; 22 Sep 2021)
Priority: Paris Convention priority may be claimed on filing. A priority document must be submitted within 3 months of the filing date. (Kluwer; 22 Sep 2021)
Opposition: Effective 16 August 2025, opposition is no longer available, but objections and invalidation actions may be submitted. (CWB, "New Trade Mark and Patent Laws Enter into Force in Albania"; 9 Sep 2025) Previously, within 9 months of the publication of the grant of the patent. (Katzarov; 22 Sep 2021)
Examination: The applicant must submit an examination report from an authorized examining authority to the Albanian Office within 10 years of the filing date. (Katzarov; 22 Sep 2021)
Duration: 20 years from the filing date. (Katzarov; 24 Sep 2021)
Working: A patent should be worked within the later of 3 years from the issue date or 4 years from the filing date, or risk compulsory license. (Kluwer; 24 Sep 2021)

The application is published at 18 months from the priority date. 4. The applicant may optionally "demand" an International Preliminary Examination (IPE) be conducted by an International Preliminary Examining Authority (IPEA). This demand triggers the Chapter II National Phase schedule. When an IPE is not demanded, the Chapter I National Phase schedule is followed.

Article 19 Amendments: Amendments may be made until the later of 16 months from the priority date or 2 months from the mailing date of the ISR and written opinion. Amendments may be accepted after the time limit if they are received before the technical preparations for international publication have been completed. (WIPO, PCT System FAQ, "Guidance on how to file amendments under Articles 19 and 34" 1.01; retrieved 24 Apr 2020)
Filing Procedure (National Phase): Effective for applications with a priority date of 1 April 2002 or later, Article 22 allows each country to extend the Chapter I National Filing deadline from the original 20 months to 30 months or longer from the earliest priority date. Most countries have extended the deadline to 30 or 31 months. Where a demand for International Preliminary Examination was filed and Chapter II was elected, National Phase entry may be made within 30 months from the priority filing date. Article 39 (1)(b) also allows for each country to set a longer deadline. Many countries have extended the deadline to 31 months.
Demand Due (Chapter II): If the applicant files a "demand" for International Preliminary Examination, entering the National Phase is based on the Chapter II schedule. As of 1 January 2004, the demand may be filed by the later of a) 3 months from receipt of the international search report and opinion or b) 22 months from the priority date. To add the 3-month demand deadline, use WO-Demand Due (Transmittal Dt) from the Action Type table.
