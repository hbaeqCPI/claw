Law Highlights
The African Regional Industrial Property Organization was created with the Lusaka Agreement. For trademarks, membership is determined by the Banjul Agreement.

After the grant of an ARIPO trademark registration, the national laws of the Contracting States apply.

Conventions: See listings for individual countries.

Filing Requirements: 1. Power of Attorney, to be submitted within 2 months of the filing date. A 1-month extension is possible. (Banjul Protocol on Marks, 2026 Edition; Rule 42 (1-2)) 2. Representation of the mark 3. Designated states 4. Declaration of actual use or intention to use the mark 5. Classification according to International class. Multi-class applications are possible (Katzarov Manual of Industrial Property; 1 Apr 2020)

Priority: Paris Convention priority may be claimed on filing. Effective 1 March 2026, a priority document in English or with English translation should be submitted within 2 months of the filing date. (Banjul Protocol on Marks, 2026 Edition; Rule 15(1)) Previously, within 3 months. (Banjul Protocol, 2023 Edition; Rule 8)

Opposition: Within 3 months from the publication of the acceptance of the application, an interested party may file an opposition with the Offices of the designated states. (Kluwer; 1 Apr 2020)

Allowance: Effective 1 March 2026, following the end of the opposition period, the Office will notify the applicant and request payment of the registration fee within 3 months of the notice. (Banjul Protocol 2026; Rule 24(1)) Previously, 12 months. (Banjul Protocol 2023, Sec.6bis:5)

Refusal: If the ARIPO Office refuses an application after a request for reconsideration, the applicant may request the application be processed as a national application in the designated states. (Banjul Agreement, Sec. 5:4; last update 2019) Effective 1 March 2026, each designated State may inform the ARIPO Office that the registration shall have no effect in the territory of that state within 6 months from the date of notification of the intention to register a trademark. (Banjul Protocol 2026; Rule 21) Previously, 9 months. (Banjul Agreement Sec. 6:2)

Duration: 10 years from the filing date, renewable for like periods. (Banjul, Sec. 7:1-2; updated 2019)
Renewal Requirements: Renewals should be submitted to the ARIPO Office in the last 12 months of the trademark term. (Katzarov; 1 Apr 2020) 6 months grace, with fine. (Banjul, Sec. 7:3)
Classification: International classification of goods and services

Law Highlights
Belarus is one of the former USSR Republics. The USSR ceased to exist from 25 December 1991, and Belarus became an independent state.

Conventions: WIPO, WTO, Paris Convention, Trademark Law Treaty, Madrid Agreement, Nice Agreement, Madrid Protocol, Singapore Treaty

Filing Requirements: 1. Power of Attorney, to be submitted when filing or within 2 months of the filing date. 2. Representation of the mark 3. List of goods and services according to Nice international classification. (Katzarov Manual of Industrial Property; last retrieved 2 Dec 2025) (Kluwer IP Law; 2 Dec 2025)

Priority: Paris Convention priority may be claimed on filing or within 2 months of the filing date. A priority document must be submitted within 3 months of the filing date. (Kluwer; 2 Dec 2025)

Opposition: Informal observations may be submitted at any time. Cancellation on relative grounds is only possible within 5 years of registration. (SMD Country Index; last retrieved 29 Jan 2016)

Allowance: The registration fee should be paid within 2 months of the decision of the Office, following which the trademark registration certificate is issued within 1 month. (Kluwer; 2 Dec 2025)

Duration: 10 years from filing, renewable for like terms. (Trademarks Throughout the World, Release #64, March 2000)

Use: Trademarks registered from 25 January 2010 may be canceled if not used for a period of 3 years from the date of registration. Previously, the period was 5 years from registration. (SMD Country Index; 29 Jan 2016)

Renewal Requirements: Applications for renewal may be filed in the year prior to expiration. 6 months grace, with fine. (Kluwer; 2 Dec 2025)

Transitional Provisions: 1. Re-registration of trademarks registered in the former USSR could be requested before 5 October 1993 to retain the filing and priority dates. The duration of the registration is 10 years from the filing date of the request. A request for such registration could not be filed after the expiration of 10 years from the original date of filing.

2. Until 5 August 1993, further processing of pending applications filed in the USSR could be requested in Belarus. Maintenance of the priority date could be requested, provided the request was filed within 21 months of the priority date.

Classification: International classification system of goods and services. Multi-class applications are possible

Types of Marks: Trademarks, service marks, collective marks, geographical indications

Trademark Law Update ___________________________

Country: Colombia (CO)

ORD - Ordinary Trademark Filing

Law Highlights

Conventions: WIPO, WTO, Paris Convention, Pan-American Convention (1929), Andean Community (Cartagena Agreement), Trademark Law Treaty, Madrid Protocol

Filing Requirements: 1. Power of Attorney, to be submitted within 2 months of the filing date. (Kluwer IP Law; retrieved 29 Jun 2023)

2. Application form with applicant and agent details 3. Representation of the mark 4. Goods and services with international class. Multi-class applications are possible. (Katzarov Manual of Industrial Property; 29 Mar 2018)

Priority: Priority may be claimed according to the Paris Convention. A certified copy of the priority document must be filed within 9 months of the priority filing. (Kluwer; 29 Mar 2018)

Opposition: Within 30 working days of the publication of the mark. (Kluwer; 29 Mar 2018)

Duration: 10 years from registration, renewable for like periods. Per a resolution effective 20 November 2017, the term for registration starts from the "the date of granting," also called "the date the granting resolution is in firm." (Pons IP; March 2018) (Resolution No. 75348 of 2017) Commercial slogans may have a term 10 years from the parent registration date. Manually adjust the first renewal by entering the renewal date in the "Next Renewal" field. Subsequent renewals should generate correctly.

Renewal Requirements: Renewal is to be applied for during the period of 6 months prior to expiration. 6 months grace. No provision for restoration. (Kluwer; 29 Mar 2018)

Use: A mark becomes vulnerable to cancellation if it has not been used for any period of 3 consecutive years following registration. Use in one of the Andean Community countries will suffice. (Kluwer; 29 Mar 2018) Prior to 13 December 1991 it was required to file evidence of use when applying for renewal.

Types of Marks: Trademarks, service marks, collective marks, trade names, commercial slogans

Transitional Provisions: Trademarks registered prior to 13 December 1991 were registered for 5 years from registration, renewable for like periods. Trademarks registered under the former law retain their 5-year duration, but are to be renewed under the new law. (Manual Industrial Property, Supplement No. 79, November 1997)

Law Highlights
The former British Colony of Cyprus became an independent Republic within the British Commonwealth on 16 August 1960.

Conventions: WIPO, WTO, TRIPS, Paris Convention, Trademark Law Treaty, Madrid Agreement, Madrid Protocol, European Union

Filing Requirements: 1. Representation of the mark 2. Indication of goods. 3. Applicant details (Katzarov Manual of Industrial Property; last retrieved 12 Oct 2016) Effective with the new regulations from June 2020, Power of Attorney is no longer required. (SMD Country Index, Newsletter Issue 11/20; 9 Jul 2020)

Priority: Paris Convention priority may be claimed on filing the application. A priority application must be submitted at the same time. If not in Greek, a Greek translation must be submitted within 2 months of filing in Cyprus. (Kluwer IP Law; 2 Dec 2025)

Opposition: Effective 18 June 2020, within 3 months from the publication of the application in the Official Gazette. (SMD Country Index News 11/20; 9 Jul 2020) (Bureau Joseph, July 2020) Previously, 2 months. (Kluwer; 12 Oct 2016)

Use: A mark becomes vulnerable to cancellation if it has not been used for 5 years. The Trademarks Law and Regulations effective June 2020 clarified that this 5-year period begins on the date registration proceedings are completed. (Koushos Korfiotis Papacharalambous, July 2020)

Duration: Effective for trademarks filed or renewed from 18 June 2020, 10 years from the filing date, renewable for like periods. (Bureau Joseph, July 2020) The "registration date" coincides with the filing date. Previously, 7 years from the filing date, renewable for 14-year periods. (Kluwer; 12 Oct 2016)

Renewal Requirements: 1. Power of Attorney 2. Application 3. Fee Renewal must be applied for within the 3-month period prior to expiration. There is a 2-month grace period, from the date the non-payment of the fee is published in the Gazette. (Kluwer; 12 Oct 2016)

Classification: International classification system of goods and services. As of 18 June 2020, multi-class applications are possible

Types of Marks: Trademarks, service marks, certification marks, house marks, trade names, defensive registrations, collective marks, color marks

ORD - Ordinary Trademark Filing

Filing Requirements: 1. Power of Attorney, legalized, to be submitted within 60 days of the filing date (Kluwer IP Law, retrieved 31 Jul 2025) 2.
